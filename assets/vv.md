# Geography and Climate

[Playa Del Carmen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Playa_del_Carmen) is a city on the Caribbean coast of Mexico, about 34 mi (55 km) south of Cancun International Airport (CUN). The city is located in the [Riviera Maya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Riviera_Maya) tourist district, in the state of [Quintana Roo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Riviera_Maya), on the [Yucatán peninsula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yucat%C3%A1n_Peninsula). 

The region has a [tropical savannah climate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tropical_savanna_climate). In April and May, temperatures in Playa Del Carmen typically vary between 70--89°F (21--32°C). This is also a relatively dry time of year: April and May each average 3--7 rainy days with 2--3 in (51--78 mm) of rainfall. The sea is also warm: water temperatures are  roughly 81--82°F (27--28°C). 

# Travel Documentation

All of you will need an unexpired passport to travel to Mexico. If you're a citizen of the US or you have a US visa, you shouldn't need anything else.

All of you will be asked to fill out a form called the FMM ("Forma Migratoria Multiple," or Multiple Immigration Form) on your flight. This is not a visa. Apparently, when you arrive in Mexico, an immigration official at the airport will stamp two parts of this document and give you the smaller part. You'll need to keep this part safe and surrender it when you leave Mexico. If you lose it, there might be a fine.

# Transportation

You'll want to fly to Cancun International Airport (CUN). 

You can take a [bus, shuttle, or taxi](https://www.cancunairport.com/transportation.html) to go between the airport and Playa Del Carmen. 

If you have a US driver's license, you can also rent a car. You can rent one at the airport for the duration of your stay (eg, at [City Car Rental](https://www.citycarrental.com/cancun-car-rental)), or you can rent one when you need it from one of several car rental agencies in PDC (eg, the [Avis](https://goo.gl/maps/Q4PXg6g1EV9k5HH49) next door to the hotel). Parking at the hotel costs 100 MXN (~ 5 USD) per night. If you rent a car, note that you will probably need to purchase Third Party Liability insurance through the car rental agency since Mexican law requires you to have this through a Mexican insurer. You are also required to have Collision Damage insurance, but your US-based credit card *might* provide this. If it does, and if you intend to decline Collision Damage through the rental agency, make sure to carry documentation.

# Activities

Here are some activities we've planned: 

* Mon 4/25: **La Cueva Del Chango**. You're all invited to dinner and drinks at this restaurant at 6pm! It's an [8-minute walk](https://g.page/lacuevadelchango?share) away from the hotel. 
    
* Tue 4/26: **Chichen Itza and Valladoid**. Chichen Itza is an archaeological site for a large Mayan city that was built perhaps as early as 750 CE. It's about a 2.5 hours drive from PDC. Valladoid is a city en route between Chichen Itza and PDC that is famous for its Mayan food, so we thought we could stop there for lunch after visiting Chichen Itza on our way back to PDC (maybe at [IX CAT IK](https://ixcatik.mx/)?). We haven't made any advance bookings for Chichen Itza or Valladoid, but Tuesday seems like a reasonable day to do this.
    
* Wed 4/27: **In-Ha Snorkel Tour**. We've chartered a catamaran for 4 hours which will take us out to the In-Ha reef, where we can hang out and/or snorkel. There should be a variety of marine life around, including sea turtles and stingrays! On board, there'll be an open bar (margaritas, beer, wine, etc), snacks (chips, guac, ceviche), snorkel gear, floaties, towels, etc. We'll meet at a [dock in Puerto Aventuras](https://g.page/RivieraElite?share) (26 minutes south of PDC) at 8:15am.

* Fri 4/29: **Xplor**. [Xplor](https://www.xplor.travel/en/) is an adventure park in PDC with zip lines, ATVs, flooded caverns, and fun things like that. If you want to go with us, you can book tickets through their website. There's a discount for booking in advance. Children under 5 can't do any of the activities, but they *can* be inside the park. 

We haven't made any other specific plans yet, but here's a list of some other things we might do. If you have any suggestions for things that you'd like to add to this list, just let us know!

* A *cenote* is a natural pool formed when a limestone cave collapses where we can hang out, swim, snorkel, and jump off of cliffs. There are several cenotes around PDC. One possibility is [Cenote Jardin del Eden](https://goo.gl/maps/yFpLwrMDc2Q5X7q77), located in Puerta Aventuras (26 minutes south of PDC). [Cenote Azul](https://goo.gl/maps/DB5wGPghbodcVApf8) and [Cenote Cristalino](https://goo.gl/maps/vrUKLmXA1LkKoN477) are also located right next to it.

* Coco Beach is a beach in PDC where we can do some snorkeling. There are, of course, lots of other beaches we can play at in PDC as well. 

* La Quinta (Fifth Avenue) is a walking street in PDC lined with shops, bars, and restaurants. There is also a [Frida Kahlo Museum](https://www.facebook.com/MuseoFKRM/) and several art galleries.

* Las Coloradas is a set of pink lakes. 

* There are Mayan ruins at Muyil. 

* We can go kayaking in Bacalar Lagoon.

Results of this test should be available in 30--60 minutes. 

# Miscellaneous

You'll certainly want to bring swim and/or beach clothes! If you bring sunscreen with you, please make sure that it's biodegradable (non-biodegradable sunscreen destroys coral reefs and is banned in much of the area). 

You might also check to see if your cell phone plan will provide coverage in Mexico. Sunny and Markus will be able to text and use data, and also make calls for 0.20 USD per minute. 

As of March 19, the currency conversion is about 21 MXN (pesos) per USD. Note that Mexico prefixes prices in pesos using the $ symbol (just like the US does for prices in dollars).
