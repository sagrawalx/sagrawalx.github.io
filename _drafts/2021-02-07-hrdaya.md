<div class="introduction">
Here is an unabashedly modified version of the shorter Prajñāpāramitāhṛdayasūtra (known in English as the "Heart Sutra"). This was mostly an exercise for me to learn some Sanskrit. 

I started with the version on [Bibliotheca Polygotta](https://www2.hf.uio.no/polyglotta/index.php?page=fulltext&view=fulltext&vid=836&cid=638829&mid=1476628). I compared it to the version on [Wikisource](https://sa.wikisource.org/wiki/%E0%A4%AA%E0%A5%8D%E0%A4%B0%E0%A4%9C%E0%A5%8D%E0%A4%9E%E0%A4%BE%E0%A4%AA%E0%A4%BE%E0%A4%B0%E0%A4%AE%E0%A4%BF%E0%A4%A4%E0%A4%BE%E0%A4%B9%E0%A5%83%E0%A4%A6%E0%A4%AF) (which agrees with the version in the [Māhāyāna Sūtra Saṃgraha, Part I](https://archive.org/details/in.ernet.dli.2015.405222/), edited by P. L. Vaidya and published in 1961 by the Mithila Institute), as well as the version in Edward Conze's [Buddhist Wisdom Books](https://archive.org/details/buddhistwisdombo00harp/), published 1958 by Harper Torchbooks. Besides Conze's translation therein, I also consulted [Śraddhāpa's translation](https://www.sraddhapa.com/hrdaya) as well as [Thich Nhat Hanh's](https://plumvillage.org/about/thich-nhat-hanh/letters/thich-nhat-hanh-new-heart-sutra-translation/) to help me with understanding parts of the Sanskrit. I then excised and modified things in order to make the whole feel concise and coherent to me. Here are some of the changes I made. 

* I removed the line which reads *pancaskandhāṃs tāṃś ca svabhāvaśūnyān paśyati sma*, since this repeats something that follows later.

* The longer version of the Heart Sutra tells a story in which the Buddha, Avalokiteshvara, and Śāriputra are all characters. Both of the short versions remove the Buddha from the story. They also essentially remove Śāriputra from the story, but Avalokiteshvara's vocative addresses to Śariputra are retained. This seems silly to me, so I excised them. 

* A number of "lists" are enumerated in this sutra. I added the usual names of these lists before their enumerations. (TO DO!)

* One part reads *na cakṣūrdhātur yāvan na manovijñānadhatuḥ*. The long ū is probably a typo. In any case, in isolation, this seems to be a condensed list to the eighteen dhātus (which starts with *cakṣurdhatu*, ie, the eye sense-dhātu, and ends *manovijñānadhatu*, the mind consciousness-dhātu). But the first twelve elements of this list (the sense-dhātus and the sense-object-dhātus) have already been listed in the sutra. I thus replaced this phrase with *na cakṣūrvijñanadhātur yāvan na manovijñānadhatuḥ*, so that what we have is a condensed list of just the six consciousness-dhātus. 
</div>
