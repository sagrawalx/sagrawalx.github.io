Here comes the procession of the Sun! (2017-10-05)

The original poem, written by Harivanish Rai Bachchan (1907-2003) in Hindustani, is called आ रही रवी की सवारी · آ رہی روی کی سواری, and comes from the collection निशा निमंत्रण · نشا نمنترن (“Invitation of the night,” 1938). The original is available in Nagari on Kavita Kosh.

I have a friend who enjoys waking up very early and watching the sunrise (sentiments I cannot understand…). I was reminded of this poem during one of our conversations, and decided to translate it — in part to share it with my friend since she does not speak Hindustani, but mostly to procrastinate.

Here comes the procession of the Sun!

The gilded chariot of newborn sunbeams,
A floral path blooming before it,
Cloud-like attendants clad in gold,
Here comes the procession of the Sun!

The birds are a convoy of minstrels,
Singing paeans into the skies,
As the Army of Stars flees the battlefield,
Here comes the procession of the Sun!

I want to leap up, proclaiming "Victory!"
But I falter when I see—
The illustrious King of the Night, now in tatters…
Here comes the procession of the Sun!
