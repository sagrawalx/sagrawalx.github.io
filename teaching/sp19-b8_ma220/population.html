Introduction

Haddock, a species of fish, live to be at most 10 years of age. They have offspring between 3 and 7 years of age.

Suppose that we record the population of haddock of all ages (in millions) in a particular area using a 10-tall vector \(v\). For example, if \(v = (100,90,80,70,60,50,40,30,20,10)\), this means that there are 100 million newborn haddock, 90 million 1 year old haddock, 80 million 2 year old haddock, and so forth.
